Chow Mein

Description

This chow mein recipe features pan-fried crispy noodles with tender beef and a variety of vegetables in a thick gravy. The sauce saturates the noodles creating a wonderful contrast of soft and crunchy noodles. Your mouth will love you.

Ingredients

  • Beef
  • Chow mein
  • Onions
  • Mushrooms
  • Red and yellow peppers
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Oyster sauce
  • Sesame oil
  • Soy sauce
  • Corn starch
  • Chicken bouillon
  • Water
  • oil

Steps

  1. Place two TBSP of oil in wok on medium heat
  2. When oil is hot, add thinly sliced chicken
  3. Cut veggies while chicken is cooking, mince garlic
  4. Remove chicken from wok when just fully cooked, and set aside
  5. Add another two TBSP of oil to pan, and add garlic, ginger, and onion. Stir frequently so garlic doesn't burn
  6. Boil a large pot of water and heat another cast iron skillet. This will be for frying the chow mein
  7. When onions are transluscent, add remaining vegetables and cover wok, stirring occasionally
  8. When water is boiled, add chow mein noodles and let cook one minute. Drain, and manually separate the noodles
  9. Add 4 TBSP of oil to cast iron skillet, and add the chow mein noodles, flipping occasionally, once crispy on bottom
  10. Add 3 TBSP oyster sauce, 1/2 TSP chicken bouillon, 1 TBSP sesame oil, and 1 TSP soy sauce to vegetables. Add cooked beef back to wok
  11. Mix 2 TBSP cornstarch with 2 TBSP cold water and mix until dissolved
  12. Add cornstarch slurry to vegetable mixture and stir constantly until thick. Remove from heat
  13. Remove chow mein from skillet, place on a serving plate, and pour vegetable mixture over chow mein
  14. Serve and enjoy
